The Problem

These whales are running out of time. And most people have no idea.

Every spring, critically endangered North Atlantic right whales come to Cape Cod Bay to feed. They surface between whale watch boats and fishing lines. They surface, exhausted, tangled in ropes they cannot see coming.

  • Over 50% of known right whale deaths come from entanglements and ship strikes
  • Only about 70 right whale females of reproductive age remain on Earth
  • Marine debris, water pollution, and shifting food sources threaten every species in the Gulf of Maine
  • Federal funding cuts are leaving frontline research programs fighting to survive
Why CCS

We don't just study these animals. We pull fishing gear off them with our bare hands.

Since 1976, the Center for Coastal Studies has operated at the intersection of science, rescue, and policy. We pioneered large-whale disentanglement. We run one of the most detailed right whale monitoring programs on Earth. And we bring 20,000 people a year into the story.

🐋

World's First Large-Whale Disentanglement Program

CCS was the first organization in the world to successfully free a large whale from fishing gear. Our rescue team is on call 24/7, 365 days a year and has freed more than 200 whales and sea turtles since 1984.

🔬

50 Years of Unbroken Scientific Research

Our humpback whale program is the longest continuously running baleen whale study in the world. In the 2023-2024 season alone, our team flew over 12,000 nautical miles of aerial surveys monitoring right whales in Cape Cod Bay.

🌊

Science That Shapes Policy

CCS researchers collaborate with NOAA, the National Marine Fisheries Service, Cornell University, and international partners to translate field data into real protections for marine animals and coastal ecosystems.

Questions

We have answers.

Where does my monthly donation actually go?
Your gift directly funds CCS research, rescue operations, and education programs. This includes aerial surveys tracking right whales in Cape Cod Bay, the 24/7 Marine Animal Entanglement Response (MAER) hotline and rescue team, water quality monitoring, and free educational programs serving 20,000+ people annually.
Can I cancel my monthly gift at any time?
Yes, absolutely. You can cancel your recurring gift at any time with no fees and no questions asked. Just contact us at ccs@coastalstudies.org or call (508) 487-3622 and we will take care of it right away.
Is my donation to CCS tax deductible?
Yes. The Center for Coastal Studies is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (Federal Tax ID: 04-2609788). All donations are tax deductible to the extent allowed by law. You will receive a receipt for your records with each gift.
How is CCS different from other marine conservation organizations?
CCS is the only organization in the world that pioneered large-whale disentanglement, and one of very few nonprofits that combines active rescue operations with rigorous scientific research and public education under one roof. We are based at the tip of Cape Cod, which gives us unparalleled access to the Gulf of Maine and the whales that depend on it.
What is the North Atlantic Right Whale Emergency Initiative?
The Right Whale Emergency Initiative is CCS's focused campaign to protect the critically endangered North Atlantic right whale, with only 336 individuals remaining. It coordinates aerial surveys, rescue response, and habitat research specifically aimed at understanding and reducing the two main threats: entanglement in fishing gear and ship strikes.
How will I know my gift is making a difference?
Watch Circle members receive monthly field update emails from CCS scientists and the rescue team. You will hear about whale sightings, entanglement rescues, water quality findings, and education milestones, with real photos and stories from the field. You will never wonder where your money went.
Can I volunteer with the Center for Coastal Studies?
Yes! CCS welcomes volunteers for beach cleanups, water quality monitoring, ambassador kiosk staffing on MacMillan Wharf, and support at the Spinnaker Exhibit and SEA Space Marine Discovery Center. Visit coastalstudies.org/get-involved/volunteers to learn more.
What happens if I give a one-time gift instead of monthly?
One-time gifts are always welcome and deeply appreciated. Monthly giving simply allows CCS to plan and sustain programs throughout the year rather than relying on one-time spikes. But any amount, given any way, makes a real difference to the mission.
Does CCS have public educational programs I can attend?
CCS offers whale walks, seal hikes, Whale Camp for Kids, the SEA Space Marine Discovery Center, the Spinnaker whale skeleton exhibit, and public lectures. Most programs are free or low cost. Programs are free to EBT, WIC, and ConnectorCare cardholders. Check the events calendar at coastalstudies.org/events.
Is CCS facing any funding challenges right now?
Like many science and conservation organizations, CCS has felt the impact of federal funding uncertainty. Monthly donors provide stable, predictable income that allows CCS to maintain its rescue team on call 365 days a year and keep critical research programs running regardless of grant cycles.
